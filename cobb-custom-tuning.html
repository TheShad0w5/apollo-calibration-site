Common COBB Tuning Problems We Help Solve.

Many drivability and boost issues are caused by torque strategy, airflow modeling, compensations, or sensor behavior—not simply ignition timing.

Boost targeting issues: throttle closure, wastegate behavior, boost oscillation, and torque intervention.
Fueling instability: MAF scaling, SD correction, transient fueling, ethanol compensation, and AFR behavior.
Drivability problems: tip-in response, throttle sensitivity, surge, hesitation, and cruise instability.
Knock correction: false knock review, ignition strategy, thermal behavior, and correction analysis.
Torque management conflicts: driver demand interaction, load limits, throttle intervention, and ECU torque modeling.
Flex fuel setup: ethanol sensor scaling, compensation strategy, fueling blending, and startup behavior.

COBB Calibration Workflow.

The process is focused on understanding the actual behavior of the ECU strategy, not simply increasing boost or ignition timing.

1. Hardware Review

Intake setup, fuel system, turbocharger, intercooler, injectors, ethanol system, and sensor package review.

2. Data Analysis

Datalog review for boost control, knock behavior, fueling correction, airflow, and torque interaction.

3. Calibration Refinement

Structured revisions for drivability, boost control, power delivery, safety systems, and repeatability.
