Common Haltech Problems We Help Diagnose.

Haltech issues can come from setup, wiring, sensor data, trigger configuration, calibration strategy, or hardware behavior. We help separate the real cause.

Trigger and timing issues: trigger pattern setup, edge selection, base timing lock, crank/cam signal behavior, and RPM instability.
Startup problems: cranking fuel, post-start enrichment, ignition timing, injector setup, and cold-start behavior.
Fueling errors: injector data, fuel pressure reference, ethanol strategy, VE behavior, transient enrichment, and sensor scaling.
DBW and idle instability: throttle setup, target control, idle airflow, startup flare, decay behavior, and control-loop conflicts.
Boost control problems: solenoid setup, wastegate behavior, closed-loop response, overboost protection, and compensation strategy.
CAN and dash issues: IC-7 integration, CAN channel mapping, termination, baud rate, warning logic, and communication faults.

The goal is to review the system cleanly, identify the bottleneck, and create a clear calibration or troubleshooting path.

2. File & Log Review

3. Support Direction

We provide setup corrections, calibration direction, diagnostic next steps, remote review, or dyno validation guidance.
