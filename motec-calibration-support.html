Common MoTeC Problems We Help Resolve.

Many MoTeC issues are caused by configuration, hardware behavior, trigger setup, or system integration—not just a bad fuel or ignition table.

Trigger and sync issues: crank/cam configuration, edge selection, ref/sync behavior, offset validation, scope review, and RPM stability.
Startup and timing problems: base timing lock, cranking fuel, injector timing, ignition phasing, and first-start troubleshooting.
Fueling strategy: injector data, fuel pressure reference, ethanol strategy, transient response, fuel model setup, and compensations.
DBW and idle control: throttle control, idle airflow, startup flare, target stability, and driver-demand behavior.
Boost control issues: wastegate control, closed-loop setup, dome pressure strategy, compensations, and safety limits.
CAN and dash communication: baud rate, termination, channel mapping, warning outputs, logging, and device communication faults.

MoTeC Support Workflow.

The process is built around reducing guesswork, validating inputs, and developing a calibration strategy that matches the actual vehicle program.

1. System Review

We review the engine package, ECU model, sensors, injectors, ignition system, boost control hardware, fuel system, and project goals.

2. Data Inspection

Logs, configurations, scope captures, CAN data, dyno data, and setup screenshots are reviewed to isolate the true issue.

3. Calibration Direction

We provide setup corrections, calibration strategy, diagnostic direction, remote support, or dyno validation guidance depending on the project.
