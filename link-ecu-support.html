Common Link ECU Problems We Help Diagnose.

Many Link ECU problems come from trigger configuration, CAN setup, hardware behavior, or calibration strategy—not simply a bad map.

Trigger and sync issues: missing-tooth setup, trigger arming thresholds, edge selection, RPM instability, and sync loss troubleshooting.
Startup instability: cranking fuel, ignition timing validation, injector timing, startup enrichment, and cold-start behavior.
Fueling problems: injector characterization, fuel pressure reference, transient enrichment, VE behavior, and ethanol strategy.
DBW and idle issues: throttle control setup, idle airflow, startup flare, throttle tracking, and idle oscillation.
Boost control instability: wastegate setup, closed-loop response, target control, overboost protection, and compensation behavior.
CAN communication faults: dash communication, OEM integration, termination resistance, baud rate, and channel mapping.
